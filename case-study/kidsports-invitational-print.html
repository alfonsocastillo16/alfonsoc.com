Client: KIDSPORTS Invitational
Project: Branding, Graphic Design, Print Design

The KIDSPORTS Invitational is an annual non-profit youth basketball tournament that provides a unique opportunity for kids to play at Matthew Knight Arena, home of the University of Oregon Ducks basketball teams. I was appointed as Creative Director for the 2014 KIDSPORTS Invitational with the goal of rebranding the event by developing a new identity. This new identity needed to reposition the KIDSPORTS Invitational brand while still remaining true to the event's values and message. After establishing the new identity digitally I then began to carry it over to printed material. New designs were needed for the apparel, program, flyers, and signage. The new designs needed to be consistent and instill a sense of professionalism in the event's audience. After several apparel design concepts we decided on moving forward with a minimalist approach with the intention of increasing the apparel's longevity. The 2014 KIDSPORTS Invitational program was made in collaberation with the operations team, it needed to be

informational and cost-effective. The end result was a tri fold brochure that covered everything that was needed and featured a design that is simple, balanced, and visually appealing. Flyers were needed to raise public awareness and sales while simultaneously raising brand awareness. The flyer designs were all consistent and also made in black and white versions. 25 signs were needed for the event which ranged over 5 different sizes. The designs needed to incorporate the new KIDSPORTS Invitational identity without overwhelming or distracting the audience. To accomplish this all of the designs featured the same typeface and used brand colors. Lastly came the design for the staff badges, something that the KIDSPORTS Invitational didn't have in previous years. The idea behind the design was that the staff badges in unison with the staff apparel would help the event reach its goal of repositioning the event's brand to a professional level worthy of a tournament played at Matthew Knight Arena.
